Template for Haidian district unified exam papers (old version)

As a “Haidianer”, you have passed at least 24 district-wide unified examinations from grade 7 to 12 (two per semester, i.e. the mid-term and the final exams). Have you ever noticed the layout design of your exam papers?

You may have noticed that the layout design is standardized and orderly throughout papers of all subjects, similar to that of NCEE papers, in large contrast to the poorly typed papers of monthly tests held by your own school. (Actually, there are many differences between the layout design of Haidian exam papers and that of the NCEE papers, which I’ll elucidate in detail in later articles. From my perspective, though being a small detail, each district should pay attention to the layout design of their unified exam papers, trying to imitate the real NCEE papers because, after all, the unified exams are targeted at NCEE, and the more simulated in each detail, the better students are prepared for the real battle, at least psychologically.)

I don’t remember when this layout was first used, perhaps it can date back to the 2000s, but I’m sure that its life (partly) ceased in the spring of 2016. Since then, the layout design of Haidian unified exam papers has changed at least three times, and it has been no longer typeset by BookMaker since the third change. (From my observation, it may have been typeset by InDesign since then.)

In commemoration of this classic layout, I reproduced a template of it recently. Here I’ll display the first 2 pages. The content is retrieved from the Chinese subject of the first mock test for the High School Entrance Examination, 2013. (source: Beijing Haidian Teachers Training College)
